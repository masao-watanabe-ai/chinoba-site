Foundational AI Technologies

AI is built not only from models, but from reasoning, generation, variation, and externalized decision structures.

Foundational AI technologies include large language models, reinforcement learning, logic systems, creative generation, cognitive orchestration, and architectures that separate generation from decision-making.

02 / Why It Matters

Why It Matters

AI systems now generate language, code, images, plans, and possible worlds — but generation alone is not a decision.

Generation Is Not Decision

AI systems can now generate language, code, images, plans, and possible worlds.

However, generation alone does not equal decision.

The central question becomes:

How do we connect AI-generated possibilities to stable, auditable, human-aligned decision structures?

03 / Core Structure

Core Structure

Eight technical surfaces beneath modern AI — from generation models to externalized decision logic.

i

Large Language Models

Statistical generators of language and structure — the dominant surface through which AI now interacts with the world.

ii

Reinforcement Learning

Learning through interaction, reward, and stabilization — a substrate for behavior, exploration, and policy formation.

iii

Generative Models

Architectures that synthesize text, images, code, and possible worlds — generation as a first-class operation.

iv

Logic & Symbolic Reasoning

Formal systems for inference, constraints, and structured deduction — where reasoning becomes auditable.

v

Prompt Systems

The interface layer between intent and generation — prompts as programmable structures, not casual instructions.

vi

Cognitive Orchestration

Coordinating generation, memory, evaluation, and variation as composed cognitive operations rather than monolithic calls.

vii

Creative Variation

Variation as a designable property — the space of alternative outputs from which selection and judgment can operate.

viii

Externalized Decision Logic

Decisions held outside the model — in runtimes, boundaries, and traces — so judgment remains stable and auditable.
